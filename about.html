20 Historical Places in India

Famous Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Red Fort, Qutub Minar, Fatehpur Sikri, Hawa Mahal, Victoria Mahal, Humayun’s Tomb, Khajuraho Temples, Jallianwala Bagh, Sanchi Stupa, Meenakshi Amman Temple, Golden Temple, Elephanta Caves, and many more.


Soaked in hundreds of years’ worth of stories, the rich culture and traditions and of course, beliefs, are some of the best historical places in India, which have attracted people from far and wide to this beautiful land. Whether it is the myriad of palaces and forts, ancient minarets, or even the stunning temples, mosques and churches, you can find historical places of all kinds, which also make it an even more exciting place to explore and learn about.


Some of the top 10 historical places in India include the famous Taj Mahal in Agra, which is also amongst the seven wonders of the world. Or, to get transported to the times of the British Raj, pay a visit to the Victoria Memorial in Kolkata, and see the opulence of the bygone eras. For a more tranquil experience, the Golden Temple of Amritsar is the best place to be, where you can not only soak in the calmness, but also see the delicate craftsmanship on stone and marble. So, if you are a travel and history buff, who wants a peek into the architectural and cultural history of this beautiful country, you must visit the following famous historical places in India.


Here is the list of best historical places in India:

01Taj Mahal, Agra

One of the most famous historical sites in India, the Taj Mahal of Agra dates back to the 17th century. It was built by Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor, in the memory of Mumtaz Mahal, his third wife. It is also the abode of the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.


The Taj Mahal has intricate carvings and is made entirely of white marble, on the southern banks of the Yamuna River. It is also one of the Seven Wonders of the World.



The Taj Mahal is an iconic mausoleum located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is widely recognized as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Here are some key facts about the Taj Mahal:

1.Construction:-The Taj Mahal was commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died while giving birth to their 14th child. Construction of the complex took approximately 22 years and involved the efforts of thousands of artisans and craftsmen.

2.Style:-The Taj Mahal is primarily built in the Persian-influenced Mughal architectural style, characterized by its symmetrical layout, ornate decorative elements, and extensive use of white marble. The complex is a fusion of Islamic, Persian, Turkish, and Indian architectural styles.

3.Design and Layout:-The Taj Mahal complex is composed of several key elements, including the main mausoleum, a large garden, a reflecting pool, and various other structures. The main mausoleum is a grand, domed structure built of white marble, with intricate marble inlays and delicate carvings.

4.Tourism and Preservation:- The Taj Mahal is a major tourist attraction in India and receives a significant number of visitors annually. To preserve its beauty and protect it from pollution and environmental damage, certain measures have been implemented, such as restricting vehicle access and monitoring air pollution levels in the vicinity.



entry fee: INR 45 (Indians), INR 1,050 (foreigners), INR 535 (SAARC and BIMSTEC), INR 200 (for every tourist who wishes to enter the mausoleum), FREE (for children below 15 years)


timings : 06:00 a.m. to 06:30 p.m. (Closed on Fridays)

02Agra Fort, UP

One of the oldest historical places in India is the Agra Fort, located in Uttar Pradesh. A true example of the stunning Mughal form of architecture, the 16th century Agra Fort was built during Akbar’s reign and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site as well.


The structure of the fort also has other different styles of architecture, along with intricate marble and sandstone artwork. It is often also called the Lal Qila by the locals.





1.Historical Significance:-Agra Fort holds great historical and architectural significance. It was originally built as a military structure by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in the 16th century. Over the years, it served as the main residence for the Mughal emperors until the capital was shifted to Delhi.

2.Architecture:-The fort is built primarily using red sandstone, giving it a distinctive reddish appearance. It encompasses a vast area of about 94 acres and is surrounded by a 70-foot high defensive wall. The fort features impressive palaces, mosques, gardens, and audience halls, showcasing a blend of Islamic and Hindu architectural styles.

3.Key Structures:-Some of the notable structures within Agra Fort include: Diwan-i-Aam: The Hall of Public Audience, where the emperor would address and interact with the general public. Diwan-i-Khas: The Hall of Private Audience, used for private meetings and discussions with important dignitaries. Jahangir Palace: Built by Akbar for his son Jahangir, it is known for its exquisite stone carvings and decorative elements. Khas Mahal: A private palace for the emperor and his family, featuring beautiful marble work and intricate designs. Musamman Burj: An octagonal tower where Emperor Shah Jahan was imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb. It offers a panoramic view of the Taj Mahal.

4.Taj Mahal View:-Agra Fort is strategically located on the banks of the Yamuna River and offers a stunning view of the Taj Mahal, which is situated just a few kilometers away. This view emphasizes the strong historical connection between the fort and the iconic mausoleum.



entry fee:INR 35 (Indians), INR 550 (Foreigners), INR 35 (SAARC and BIMSTEC), FREE (for children below the age of 15 years)


timings : 06:00 a.m. to 06:00 p.m.

03Agra Fort, UP

When it comes to the famous historical places in India, one cannot miss the Red Fort in Delhi. Also popularly known as the Lal Qila, the Red Fort was constructed during Shah Jahan’s reign in the 17th century. Constructed using huge red sandstone walls in the Mughal style of architecture, the highlights of the Red Fort include its beautiful geometric gardens, magnificent balconies and the opulent entertainment halls.


The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort complex located in Old Delhi, India. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city and a popular tourist attraction. Here's some information about the Red Fort:





1.History:-The Red Fort was constructed in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan, who is also known for building the Taj Mahal in Agra. It was completed in 1648 and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

2.Architecture:-TThe fort is an impressive example of Mughal architecture, combining elements of Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles. It is made of red sandstone, giving it its distinctive appearance. The fort is octagonal in shape, with walls spanning about 2.5 kilometers (1.5 miles) and reaching a height of up to 33 meters (108 feet).

3.Layout:- The Red Fort is divided into several sections, including the Lahore Gate, Delhi Gate, Chatta Chowk (a covered market), Naubat Khana (Drum House), Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), Rang Mahal (Palace of Colors), Mumtaz Mahal, and the famous Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque).

4.Independence Day Celebrations:- Every year on August 15th, the Red Fort plays a prominent role in India's Independence Day celebrations. The Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag and delivers a speech from the ramparts of the fort, addressing the nation.



entry free INR 35 (Indians), INR 500 (Foreigners);


timings :09:30 a.m. to 04:30 p.m. (Closed on Mondays)

04 Qutub Minar, Delhi

Delhi’s Qutub Minar ranks amongst the top 10 historical places in India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Qutub Minar, also known as the Victory Tower, is the tallest brick minaret in the whole world, standing tall at a height of 72.5 metres. Built of marble and red sandstone, you can also see many beautiful carvings and intricate designs of the Qutub Minar.


The Qutub Minar was built in the late 1100s, to celebrate Muslim Dominance in Delhi, after its last Hindu ruler was defeated. The construction was commenced by Qutab-ud-din Aibak, followed by his successors Iltutmish and Firoz Shah Tughlak.





1.History:-Qutub Minar was built in the early 13th century, starting in 1192, by Qutub-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate. It was initially constructed as a victory tower to celebrate the Muslim dominance in Delhi. Later, additional stories were added by different rulers, including Iltutmish and Firoz Shah Tughlaq, resulting in the current structure

2.Architecture:- Qutub Minar is a towering minaret made primarily of red sandstone and marble. It stands at a height of approximately 73 meters (240 feet) and consists of five distinct stories or tiers, each marked by a projecting balcony. The first three stories are made of red sandstone, while the fourth and fifth stories are made of marble and sandstone.

3.Layout:- Qutub Minar is an excellent example of Indo-Islamic architecture, combining elements of Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles. The intricate carvings and calligraphy on the walls of the minaret showcase the mastery of the craftsmen of that era.

4.Qutub Festival:- Qutub Minar serves as the backdrop for the annual Qutub Festival, a cultural event organized by the Government of Delhi. The festival showcases classical music and dance performances by renowned artists and attracts both locals and tourists. Visiting Qutub Minar allows you to explore the rich history, impressive architecture, and cultural significance of this ancient monument. It remains a popular tourist attraction and a testament to Delhi's vibrant past.



entry free INR 10 (Indians), INR 250 (Foreigners); FREE (for children below 15 years)


timings : 07:00 a.m. to 05:00 p.m.

05 Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh

An architectural gem and one of the most exquisite historical places in India is Fatehpur Sikri, a town in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh. A recognized UNESCO World Heritage Site, Fatehpur Sikri is made predominantly out of red sandstone, and dates back to the late 16th century, when it was founded by Mughal Emperor Akbar. Fatehpur Sikri is completely fortified, and as of now, serves as the abode of Jodha Bai’s Palace, Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, Ibadat Khana, as well as the Tomb of Salim Chishti and other attractions.


Fatehpur Sikri is a historical city located in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh, India. It was founded in the 16th century by Emperor Akbar, the third Mughal emperor of India. Fatehpur Sikri served as the capital of the Mughal Empire from 1571 to 1585, during Akbar's reign.





1.History:-The city was constructed using red sandstone and showcases a unique blend of Mughal, Persian, and Indian architectural styles. It was designed as a planned city with various palaces, mosques, public buildings, and gardens. Fatehpur Sikri is renowned for its well-preserved structures that offer insights into the grandeur and opulence of the Mughal era.



entry free:- INR 40 (Indians), INR 550 (Foreigners); INR 20 (SAARC and BIMSTEC), FREE (for children below 15 years)


Timing:-06:00 a.m. to 06:00 p.m.

06 Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh

Hawa Mahal in Jaipur is one of the best historical places in India, in addition to being a prominent landmark of Rajasthan. Dating back to the 18th century, Hawa Mahal is a 5-storey structure that overlooks the bustling streets of Jaipur. It has also been made in a blend of the Rajput, Mughal and Islamic styles of architecture.


Built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the Hawa Mahal has been constructed in a way such that the small windows here allow winds to enter the palace and keep it cool during the blazing hot summers.



entry free:-INR 50 (Indians), INR 200 (Foreigners)


Timing:- 09:00 a.m. to 04:30 p.m.

07 Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh

Ranking amongst the top 10 historical places in India, Kolkata’s Victoria Memorial was constructed in honour of Queen Victoria, making it one of the relics of the British Raj. Built entirely out of white marble, the Victoria Memorial is almost a replica of its namesake in London.


Surrounded by a well-manicured garden on all sides, it is home to a wide number of sculptures, artwork, statues and more. You can also find a 16-foot-tall bronze statue of the Angel of Victory on top of the central dome of the Victoria Memorial..



entry free:-INR 30 (Indians), INR 500 (Foreigners); INR 100 (SAARC and BIMSTEC), FREE (for school children and army veterans)


Timing:- 11:00 a.m. to 05:00 p.m. (Closed on Mondays)
